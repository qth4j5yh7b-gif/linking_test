What is a Kiwi?

The kiwi, also known as kiwifruit, is a small, oval fruit with brown fuzzy skin and bright green flesh. Inside, it contains tiny black seeds and has a refreshing, sweet-tart flavor.

Origins

Kiwifruit originally comes from China but became widely popular after being grown extensively in New Zealand. Today, kiwi is enjoyed all around the world.

Health Benefits

Kiwi is rich in vitamin C, fiber, and antioxidants. Eating kiwi can support the immune system, aid digestion, and contribute to overall good health.

How to Eat Kiwi

Kiwi can be eaten on its own, added to fruit salads, blended into smoothies, or used as a topping for desserts. Some people even eat the skin for extra fiber.
