What is a Kākāpō?

The kākāpō is a rare, flightless parrot native to New Zealand. It is known for its moss-green feathers, owl-like face, and calm personality.

Unique Characteristics

Unlike most parrots, the kākāpō is nocturnal and cannot fly. It uses strong legs to climb trees and glides down instead of flying. Kākāpō are also one of the longest-living birds in the world.

Diet

Kākāpō are herbivores and eat plants, seeds, fruits, and leaves. They have a special fondness for native New Zealand plants.

Conservation

The kākāpō is critically endangered due to habitat loss and introduced predators. Dedicated conservation efforts, including protected islands and careful monitoring, are helping slowly increase their population.
